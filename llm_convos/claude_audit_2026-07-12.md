## 5. Corrections (C11–C16)

**C11 — Theorem 4 of `06` asserts announcement wrongness that its hypotheses don't quite purchase (precision fix, one or two sentences).** The procedure "announces `supported forever` or `refuted forever`," which as written is a claim about the future *atom trajectory* — a data-dependent object — while the proof's key line, "On `E_n`, the announcement is wrong under `P_+`," treats it as a claim about the *regime* (`θ` versus `ε`). These coincide only under extra assumptions: absent coverage under `P_+`, the intervals could pathologically sit below `ε` forever, making the trajectory literally supported-forever even though `θ(P_+) > ε`, and the announcement would then not be wrong on all of `E_n`. Two clean fixes, either sufficient: (a) define the announcement as the regime claim — the statement's own phrase "the corresponding correct side" suggests this was the intent — and define error accordingly; or (b) keep the trajectory reading and add the hypothesis that under each law, coverage and shrinkage hold almost surely, so that by Theorem 3 the trajectory a.s. eventually matches the regime and a wrong-regime announcement is a.s. eventually wrong. While there, make the adaptedness explicit in the statement (τ a stopping time, the declaration `F_τ`-measurable); the proof already uses exactly this via `E_n ∈ F_n`, so it costs one clause.

**C12 — Theorem 3 of `06`: the proof selects `N` from the diameter condition only.** Hypothesis 1 grants coverage only for "every sufficiently late `n`," so `N` should be the maximum of the coverage-onset index and the diameter index. One word ("both").

**C13 — Theorem 10 of `08` uses the schema-level symbol for a request-local claim.** `07` §8 reserves unsuperscripted `>=_prof` for the uniform schema relation (with the WF-transfer clause quantified over all bases), but Theorem 10 fixes a finite address set and argues at "a common well-formed base" — i.e., it proves the equivalence for `>=_prof^beta`. Either superscript the theorem's relation or state the fragment as base-fixed up front. As written, a careful reader can construct a spurious gap: schema-level failure via a WF-transfer failure at some *other* base, with no separating model at the common base. The proof is right; the statement's symbol is one level too strong.

**C14 — `kernel.assess_relative_undefeated` cannot express the invalid-search-trace case.** `07` §5.2 makes `RelUndom` supported only "when the declared search trace is valid and none exists," and open "when that trace is missing/invalid." The helper supports whenever no dominators are passed; trace validity is implicit in the caller's decision to call it, and no witness fixture exercises the invalid-trace → open path. Add a `search_trace_valid: bool = True` parameter (open with an `InvalidSearchTrace` obstacle when false) and one test. Same applies to `assess_certified_undominated`. This matters more than it looks: the relative/certified distinction is one of the project's signature moves, and its executable form currently encodes only half of the relative clause.

**C15 — `Diagnostic` enforces presence but not exclusivity of payloads.** `07` §5.3 says the diagnostic "is exactly one of" Support/Open/Refute; the dataclass is a product type whose `__post_init__` checks that the matching payload is nonempty but not that the others are empty. `Diagnostic(atom, SUPPORTED, support=("w",), obstacles=("x",), provenance=...)` currently validates. Emulate the sum type: assert the two non-matching payloads are empty. Cheap, and it protects the "lossless indexed diagnostics" claim the tests rely on.

**C16 — Two Task 14 regression "witnesses" are tautologies.** `test_spurious_impact_path_does_not_preclude_invariance` asserts two hardcoded booleans and touches nothing; `test_component_adequacy_does_not_imply_composite_adequacy` is standalone arithmetic. Both are honest as documentation (the module docstring says they aren't proof substitutes), but they inflate the headline test count and, more importantly, the first one *could* be a real kernel test at the same cost: build two `EpistemicState`s differing by one bookkeeping provenance node, assert both assessments identical, and assert the impact-graph path exists — which is precisely Countertheorem 6 executed. Recommend upgrading rather than deleting.

Minor notes, no numbers assigned: (i) `assess_request` raising `KeyError` on a missing required diagnostic is *consistent* with `07` §5.1's totality axiom (a state without the diagnostic is not a valid model), but the distinction between "missing evidence → open atom" (a semantics fact) and "missing diagnostic record → invalid fixture" (a modeling contract) deserves one comment line in the kernel, because a future fixture author will conflate them. (ii) Countermodel 13's realizability is stipulated for an abstract fragment; since I verified the concrete core realizes all seven vectors (example above), one sentence exhibiting such an instance would make the countermodel self-contained against the actual atom clauses.

## 6. Structural critiques and Checkpoint B advisory (S6–S9)

**S6 — The update cluster's flagship theorem is not yet about this calculus; prove the locality lemma that makes it so (the one proof repair I would gate Checkpoint B on).** Theorem 5 of `08` is correct, but examine what it is: "change-complete" is defined as *the graph misses no real influence* and "path-realizable" as *every reachable observed coordinate is actually changed by some allowed path* — which makes sufficiency a two-line contradiction and necessity definitionally immediate. The real content is Countertheorem 6's moral about static overapproximation. That is honest work, but as stated the cluster is a lemma about arbitrary labeled transition systems plus an *interface stipulation* (`07` §10.2's `Dep_s(r,a)` "names the support... on which its diagnostic depends" — asserted, never proved of the actual atom clauses). The missing piece is small and provable:

> **Reads-from/locality lemma.** For every operational clause in `07` §5.2, the value `ν_s(e,q,a)` is a function of the restriction of `s` to `Dep_s(r,a)` — i.e., invariant under any state change outside the dependency set — where `Dep` is defined canonically from what the clause reads (current certificate regions, validity flags, evaluated set, search trace). **Corollary.** The canonical impact graph (event schema → written record locations → atoms reading them) is change-complete for `O_val` over every update class generated by the declared event schemas of `07` §10.1.

The proof is a case analysis over the five clause families and the event schemas — an afternoon, not a research program. With it, Theorem 5's sufficiency direction becomes a theorem *of the license calculus* ("the calculus's own dependency tracking is a sound frame rule"), which is what the paper needs the cluster to be; realizability correctly remains instance-relative. Without it, a referee can say the characterization holds for any system that stipulates its own bookkeeping is accurate. Note this lemma is also exactly the ground truth Task 16's atom-supervision architecture assumes when it trains per-atom heads on dependency-scoped features, so it pays twice.

**S7 — Task 12 has a positioning debt: zero citations in the file, and two of its theorems restate classical patterns.** Theorem 1 is the standard locality/indistinguishability argument; Theorem 4 is a version of the classical impossibility of finite-time zero-error decision between hypotheses whose finite-prefix laws are mutually absolutely continuous — the pattern underlying "decidable with certainty" versus "verifiable in the limit" in formal learning theory. The literature map *knows* this (`Kelly1996` is in the bib, and the map's own guidance reads "Use Gold/Kelly to define a meta-level sequence and distinguish eventual stability from certifiable finality"), but `06_open_endedness.md` never says it, and its §14 audit table classifies Theorems 1 and 4 as contributions without a "classical pattern, restated in our setting" column. Contrast `08a` §9, which disclaims novelty over the underlying mathematics explicitly — `06` should match that posture. Concretely: cite Kelly 1996 at Theorems 1 and 4 (optionally adding Wald 1947 or a sequential-analysis survey for the measure-theoretic version — the literature map's inherited-bibliography audit trail explicitly records "Wald omitted," a Task 6 decision worth revisiting for exactly this theorem); keep Proposition 6's directionality, Corollary 3's profile transport, and the regime-asymmetry table of §10 flagged as the project-specific content, because they are. This costs a paragraph and removes the single most predictable referee objection to the stability cluster.

**S8 — The honest tally against the three-cluster bar, and what Checkpoint B should decide.** The A1 amendment requires "at least three paper-carrying results from distinct clusters," counted with definitions and standard lemmas excluded, and instructs Checkpoint B to add a focused proof repair if the spine is thin.
