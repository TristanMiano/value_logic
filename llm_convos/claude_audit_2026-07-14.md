## 5. Corrections (C17–C19)

Three items, all attribution/positioning, zero mathematical errors. This is the corpus's own standard applied to its newest files, and each is a paragraph or less.

**C17 — Theorem 7's "minimal quotient" clause is self-referential in its consumer family; add the caveat sentence and do not headline it.** The theorem proves `R = (r₁..rₙ, z₁..zₙ)` minimal for a consumer family *defined to contain every coordinate projection of `R`* — under that hypothesis, "any sufficient code determines `R`" is close to definitional (the family separates `im(R)` by construction). **C18 — Task 14B's machinery should attribute its own classical patterns, by the corpus's own S7 standard.** Two items. (i) The collection-index-key device for negative reads — "an index key is read even when its collection is empty," so that insertions must write the index — is precisely the classical solution to the **phantom problem** in database concurrency control: predicate/index locking, Eswaran, Gray, Lorie & Traiger, "The Notions of Consistency and Predicate Locks in a Database System," *CACM* 19(11):624–633, 1976.
