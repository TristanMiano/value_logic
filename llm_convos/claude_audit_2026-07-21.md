## 6. The Checkpoint D question: how much rigor does the bridge need first? (S15)

The author asks whether the claims around the `policy_value_isomorph` bridge — approximate reconstruction of a policy from a value network with a decision harness and vice versa, behavioral near-identity on the training set, bounded same-way generalization — should be refined before other work proceeds. **Answer: yes, by exactly one short formal task before or alongside Task 26, and by nothing empirical.** The reasoning, then the specification, then the boundary.

### 6.1 Where the current corpus leaves the claim

Tasks 22/22A/23 correctly dismantled the two ends of the inherited claim: the finite existence encoding is proved but semantically thin (Task 22 says so itself — "a representation theorem, not yet a theorem that the scalar has return … semantics"), and the strong identification (unique/natural/standard-return inverse, occupancy-as-utility, complexity necessity) is refuted with the right counterexamples and literature. What sits **between** them — the author's operative claim, stated in his Checkpoint D brief and in the May post's reconstruction section — is *quantitative behavioral round-trip agreement*: decode the fitted surrogate and get back the frozen policy's choices, up to a stated error, on a stated distribution. That claim currently exists only as scattered scope prose (Task 22 §2.2's greediness/tie conditions; Task 23's "behavioral fidelity" axis; `C05`'s "substantial part"). No document states it as a proposition with a bound. This is the gap: the paper's §8 presently offers one trivial positive result, one standard-mathematics information bound, and a wall of correct negatives — thin formal content for the section that carries the author's founding motivation.

### 6.2 Why closing it is nearly free: the corpus already owns the geometry

The ml/03 conservative-decoding result — sound decoding under accepted statistic-error bounds, with **exact recovery outside the `2ρ_err` ideal-margin band** — is a triangle-inequality statement about decisions decoded from perturbed statistics. The bridge's agreement claim is the *same statement* with the margin instantiated as the **action gap**. Concretely, a Task 22B (one to two pages plus a small verification test) should state:

1. **Representation round trip, arbitrary policy (the distillation variant).** Fix the Task 22 §2.1 score encoder (indicator scores for a deterministic `π`, or logits/probabilities for a stochastic one) and a tie-broken `argmax` decoder. If a fitted surrogate `Û` satisfies `‖Û(s,·) − U_π(s,·)‖_∞ ≤ ρ` on the evaluated states, then the decoded policy equals `π` exactly on every state whose top-two score gap exceeds `2ρ`; and for an evaluation distribution `μ`, occupancy-weighted disagreement is at most `μ(gap ≤ 2ρ)` plus the mass on which the error bound fails. This is the author's "behaviorally near-identical on the training set" at theorem grade, for **any** frozen policy, with no return semantics claimed — margin-certified distillation, and it should be named as such.
2. **Return-semantic round trip (the `Q^π` variant).** The identical bound for `Q̂` versus `Q^π`, with the already-adjudicated caveat carried on its face: agreement with `π` itself additionally requires `π` to be greedy with respect to its own `Q^π` there (compatible ties/perspective); elsewhere the decode is policy improvement, per Task 22 §2.2 unchanged. This variant is the one that carries return meaning and the one the companion actually implements.
3. **The license wiring, which is the point.** The action gap *is* an adequacy margin in the calculus's own sense. `P_surrogate-rely`'s `Adeq` atom instantiates as "certified gap surplus exceeds `2ρ_cert`," with abstention below — Task 23's profile gets its first concrete, checkable atom, and the bridge's agreement guarantee becomes a license rather than a hope. This is the sentence that tightens the lead question's answer: *the same margin geometry that carries the calculus's licenses carries the policy–value bridge's behavioral guarantee.* One sentence in main §8 plus the statement and short proof in Appendix F keeps the frozen 500-word budget intact, and the addition is legitimate under Task 25's own closing rule because it arrives with a completed proof.

### 6.3 The generalization clause: demote, cite, and park

"Might generalize the same way a trained model generalizes, in a bounded, limited sense" cannot currently be made a theorem, and the strong reading has known negative pressure: two learners matched on training data need not generalize alike (underspecification), and per-state disagreement `ε` compounds along trajectories — `O(T²ε)` for pure cloning, `O(Tε)` with interaction — which is the controlling formal frame for round-trip behavior in sequential settings. Task 22B should therefore (a) name a **bounded generalization-similarity conjecture** with the defensible surrogate statements (held-out on-distribution agreement via standard bounds; trajectory-level divergence per the compounding results), (b) cite the frame (L11), and (c) allocate its empirical test to the companion **after** its open PyTorch-migration tasks 23–29 complete, under the companion-gating discipline of S16. It does not enter the paper as a claim.

### 6.4 The missing motivation paragraph: the semantics-backward north star

The author's stated reason the bridge matters — recover semantic meaning at the value output first, then use the license machinery to work *backward through the network's layers* — appears nowhere in the corpus as an articulated program element. Its nearest relatives are ledger `F25` ("adequacy of adequacy" hidden layers, `T0` semantic alignment) and limitation rows `L24-P2/P3`, which supply exactly the right boundaries. One recorded-motivation paragraph (same provenance discipline as §1A: attributed, dated, not evidence) in Task 23 and a sentence in outline §1.5 or §10's future work would make the paper's motivation match the author's actual program: *value output as the first certified semantic foothold; inward propagation as named, unexecuted future work, gated by the representational-alignment and causal-faithfulness axes.* Costs nothing, claims nothing, and explains to the reader why an "optional" section exists at all.

### 6.5 What not to do, and why

- **No empirical bridge study and no companion runs before the paper.** C1 §7's no-insert contract governs; the companion's migration tasks are open, its tests were deliberately not rerun at Task 22, and the project's own preregistration standards — correctly — make "quick" empirical work impossible. A bridge experiment done properly is a Task-19-class undertaking and belongs after publication, versioned and prospectively frozen.
- **No pursuit of uniqueness, naturality, or canonical-value results.** The Skalse-class invariances and the planner/reward decomposition make that a research program, not a task; Task 22's `X1` on the strengthening is the durable answer.
- **Retire "isomorphism" from public prose** beyond one historical-motivation sentence, per the `C01` ledger instruction ("retain … only with its objects, decoder, and preserved structure stated"). The word is doing no work the two round-trip variants don't do better, and it invites exactly the referee reading the counterexample section then has to fight.
