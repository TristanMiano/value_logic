## Durable checkpoint result

The compact calculus is coherent enough to support a neural correspondence, and its mathematical center is no longer a record schema. The paper-level object remains the request `(s,e,q,P)` over the three principal carriers `E,Q,S`, with `W` semantic, `WF` separate from meaningful `K_3` atom values, and profiles supplying finite consumer queries. The quantitative atlas/router machinery remains an extension.

The theorem bar is nevertheless only conditionally cleared. The profile soundness/relative-completeness cluster is a genuine logic result. The open-ended stability package is usable after small precision fixes and explicit attribution of its classical indistinguishability and sequential-decision patterns. The update-persistence characterization does not yet count as a theorem of the concrete calculus: its graph assumptions currently stipulate accurate influence without proving that the frozen atom clauses induce such a graph. A new Task 14B will close exactly this gap and repair the executable/reference-level defects found by the audit. This original checkpoint decision commissions no broader expansion *inside Task 14B*; the later author amendment deliberately adds the separate, bounded Task 14C.

The neural target is architecture-neutral and smaller than the complete formalism. A learned component should supply dependency-scoped atom sufficient statistics and calibrated uncertainty; exact metadata, `WF`, profile roles, `K_3` aggregation, active-set masking, fallback, and provenance identity normally remain symbolic or external. ReLU remains the reference witness for continuous piecewise-linear statistics. Monotone/lattice and hard mixture-of-experts models remain the leading comparators for monotonicity and discontinuous seams. The author amendment gives a graph/set model one concrete plan-DAG hypothesis, but Task 15 must operationalize it before Checkpoint C decides whether it merits training.

Task 17 is the next prospective neural mathematical centerpiece. Its target statements are fixed below before Tasks 15–16 choose encodings. A subsequent project-author amendment adds a prior formal target, Task 14C: finite proof-carrying recursive plans, a precise account of theoremhood inside the value logic, and stratified assessment of the value-logic system itself.

The amendment also accepts a positive version of the author's dual-use ReLU intuition. A channel can simultaneously express positive certificate-relative adequacy slack and serve as a downstream numerical feature when its preactivation has an explicit, certificate-valid margin semantics—or a verified approximation envelope with sufficient boundary separation—and the later computation is defined to consume that grade. This is a conditional construction, not an interpretation of arbitrary hidden activations. General payloads, full diagnostics, and proof evidence require more than the one rectified scalar.

## What the neural layer must and need not represent

The semantic interface should factor as:

```text
typed request and registry data
    -> atom address plus dependency-scoped input x(s,e,q,a)
    -> learned or verified sufficient statistics Z_a
    -> fixed atom decoder and indexed diagnostic
    -> profile query and K_3 meet
    -> four-way outcome, active set, selection or fallback.
```

### Required learned or estimated quantities

Only quantities not already mechanically available should be learned. Depending on the selected fragment, these may include:

- risk, regret, cost, robustness, or certificate-region estimates;
- calibrated error regions or validity estimates for empirical certificates;
- pairwise comparison statistics when they cannot be computed from certified records;
- selection scores after the licensed mask is applied; and
- theorem-indexed extension quantities such as selected-scope risk, misroute severity, bridge disagreement, or plan-component sensitivities.

For a scalar certificate region `[L,U]` and tolerance `epsilon`, a natural pair is

```text
support slack = epsilon - U,
refute slack  = L - epsilon.
```

The external decoder supports when the first slack is nonnegative, refutes when the second is positive, and otherwise leaves the atom open. Missingness, invalidity, conflict, and scope mismatch stay explicit; they are not negative margins. If estimated statistics have a certified error `delta`, the safe interval is enlarged before decoding, and the boundary band is conservatively open.

### Symbolic or external structure

The default design keeps the following outside the learned scorer:

- `WF` and `WFDiag` when type/denotation/executability are mechanically decidable;
- atom address, exact scope, loss/criterion, certificate mode, profile slot, and safety/report role;
- profile instantiation and finite `K_3` meet;
- evaluated-set/search identity and exact trace-verifier results;
- provenance graph and registry identifiers;
- active-set masking, the ban on selecting an unlicensed plan, and empty-set fallback;
- library expansion and model/version storage; and
- direct composite certification or a symbolic Task 14A propagation calculation.

This division is a default, not a ban on learned validity or routing. When such a quantity is learned, it becomes a new evidence-bearing model with calibration and provenance rather than an invisible replacement for the semantic field.

### Consumer-relative compression

For a fixed profile query family `F`, exact status prediction needs to separate the classes of `V / ~_F` and the required well-formedness observations. A full audit interface is finer: it preserves address-indexed witnesses, counterwitnesses, obstacles, safety projections, and provenance pointers, as well as possible future profile queries.

Two qualifications are now mandatory:

1. `ceil(log_2 |V / ~_F|)` is a lower bound for a finite discrete code, not a lower bound on neural output width. One unrestricted real coordinate can name finitely many classes unless precision, robustness, noise, or decoder restrictions are imposed.
2. `Ill(WFDiag)` versus `Well([v]_F)` is a canonical typed normal form. An arbitrary sufficient code need only decode to, or refine, that quotient; it need not literally use a tagged datatype internally.

The coordinate-indispensability theorem similarly says that the assessment must remain sensitive to distinctions carried by `s,e,q,P`; it does not require four neural modules or four embedding blocks.

## Project-author amendment: recursive proof-carrying computation

The project author designates two ambitious questions as general goals:

1. Can an evaluated model be a finite recursive program of object choices, frames, formulations, solvers, loss estimators, and actions that both computes an output and carries compositional adequacy evidence?
2. What is a theorem *inside* value logic, and can the calculus issue a finite-stage license for an implementation of itself without collapsing into circular self-authorization?

These questions refine the earlier finite-plan-DAG clarification. The existing reification theorem only says a terminating typed DAG can be treated as one plan, while Task 14A propagates one family of numerical errors. It does not yet construct proof/certificate terms for every plan constructor or explain internal derivability and system-level assessment.

### Annotated computations rather than an overloaded scalar

The strongest general interface is a certificate-carrying result

```text
(y_hat, b, pi)
```

or, suggestively,

```text
Sigma(y_hat : Y). Sigma(b : B). Cert_q(p,x,y_hat,b).
```

Here `y_hat` is the computational payload, `b` is a quantitative grade such as an error bound, risk-to-go, cost, or vector of these, and `pi` is checkable evidence that the declared bound/contract holds under its named mode, scope, assumptions, and version. The grade `b` can be processed downstream and compared with an acceptable set. The certificate is `pi`, not the number `b` by itself.

For every primitive plan, a certified executor should return this bundle. For each finite plan constructor `c`, the formal target is to supply:

1. an output transformer;
2. a grade transformer `Phi_c`;
3. a certificate transformer taking accepted component evidence to evidence for the composite contract; and
4. explicit frame, interface, termination, scope, and provenance side conditions.

Structural induction, or topological induction for a DAG with sharing, should then prove a **proof-carrying recursive-plan theorem**: every finite well-founded plan built from certified primitives has a certified executor whose proof erasure is its ordinary computation. A further **license-lifting corollary** supports the composite adequacy atom when the root certificate matches the exact request and places the composite grade in `Acc_q`; the remaining profile atoms must still be assessed normally.

This composes contracts into a new composite certificate. It does not meet component grants into a composite grant. Task 14A's path-sensitivity bound is one intended grade/certificate transformer, including its perturbation-tube and resource-accounting assumptions.

### The image-classifier/ReLU example

Suppose channels are explicitly indexed by hypotheses such as

```text
flower, dog, cat, food, person, child, house, ...
```

and a common context gives each a commensurate signed, certificate-valid adequacy margin `m_i(x)`. For example, if `U_i(x)` is an accepted upper loss bound valid for the declared mode and scope, `epsilon_i` is the request tolerance or status-quo-relative acceptance boundary, and `sigma_i>0` fixes common units, one may use the normalized surplus

```text
m_i(x)=(epsilon_i-U_i(x))/sigma_i.
```

Then

```text
z_i(x)=ReLU(m_i(x))
```

has a legitimate dual role:

- `z_i>0` witnesses positive certificate-relative slack above the chosen boundary; and
- the vector `z(x)` is a nonnegative numerical feature vector available to later layers.

A vector such as `(0,0.2,0.2,0,5,3,0.1,...)` may therefore encode that the `person` hypothesis has the largest **positive normalized surplus** among the named channels, and later computation may use that surplus. This realizes the author's intuition under explicit semantics.

Four qualifications are essential:

1. Raw hidden activations do not acquire this meaning merely from being ReLU values. The channel-to-hypothesis map, target statistic, units, calibration, domain, and certificate validity must be established. If `m_i` is only an estimate, a verified error envelope and adequate boundary separation are required; world-level adequacy additionally needs the named soundness bridge.
2. `argmax_i z_i` means “largest displayed surplus” only when margins are commensurate. It need not mean lowest risk, greatest posterior probability, or globally best model.
3. With the inclusive support convention `m_i>=0`, `ReLU(m_i)=0` conflates boundary support with every negative margin. Exact status still needs the signed preactivation, paired negative channel, or a symbolic support/state bit; open/refuted and missing/invalid evidence also remain separate.
4. Two plans can have the same adequacy margin but different predictions, costs, or traces. An adequacy scalar is not generally a sufficient computational payload.

The exact abstraction begins with a **joint-sufficiency factorization condition**. Let `F` be the required license-query family and `C` the required downstream computation. A representation `z` is sufficient for both exactly when

```text
z(omega)=z(omega')
```

implies both equal `F`-query answers and equal `C` outputs. Equivalently, the code equivalence induced by `z` refines the intersection of the license-query and computation equivalences. This equivalence is a definition-level characterization, not by itself a paper-carrying theorem. Task 17 must add a nontrivial construction or minimal quotient/factorization result for a specified channel family, plus an obstruction whenever equal adequacy margins hide different payloads.

Using margin magnitude downstream also creates a scale obligation. License status may be invariant under a joint positive rescaling of loss, tolerance, and certificate bounds while `z` changes. A downstream computation using `z` must be covariant to that transformation or declare normalized, unit-bearing margins. Multiplying a model output by `ReLU(m)` is not a free gate: it suppresses boundary-supported outputs, scales content by margin units, and defines a new blended plan. The safe default keeps content and grade channels separate and uses a symbolic router; score-as-content remains an explicitly selected special case.

### Four meanings of “theorem”

The paper must distinguish:

1. **Object-model result:** a model, equation system, or prover produces `[e,q] phi`, optionally with a proof object in a named formal theory. The label is not erased.
2. **Internal value-logic derivation:** `Gamma ; s |-VL J` derives a license, diagnostic, or authorized labeled output from typed premises and checked evidence.
3. **Metatheorem:** an externally proved soundness, completeness, locality, composition, representation, or impossibility result about value logic.
4. **Empirical certificate judgment:** a confidence region, calibration trace, test record, or learned bound is accepted under a certificate mode and operationally supports an atom.

Curry–Howard is a useful guide for the second level and for proof terms checked inside a named formal theory. It is not an unrestricted identification of neural scores or empirical support with truth. A checker acceptance can establish a syntactic judgment under its checker-correctness assumptions; a world-level fact still requires the declared soundness bridge. A learned confidence interval can inhabit an “accepted certificate under mode `m`” type without becoming a deductive proof of `Target_w(a)`.

### Licensing the value-logic system

A frozen implementation of the encoder, scorer, decoder, router, or proof checker can itself be reified as a plan `e_VL` and assessed by an ordinary higher-order request

```text
(s_meta, e_VL, q_meta, P_meta).
```

The meta-context must fix what is being evaluated: decoder fidelity, false-grant/false-refusal loss, calibration, routed risk, computational resources, derivation checking, or a stronger formal correctness contract. Evidence must come from typed base premises, independent checks, a higher-ranked verifier, or an explicitly sound certificate transformer. The system's own assertion of adequacy is not its sole evidence.

Task 14C will seek two finite results:

- **Grounded-provenance theorem.** In a finite acyclic support graph, every supported required atom has a provenance path to an explicitly typed base source. A closed mutually supporting component is syntactically impossible in this DAG fragment rather than excluded by the theorem.
- **Stratified self-evaluation theorem.** If evaluator/request dependencies admit a finite rank, every evaluative edge points to lower rank or exogenous evidence, and every local evaluator is total and deterministic on its typed inputs, diagnostics and assessments are uniquely determined by induction. Reifying `e_VL` preserves this property.

These results permit finite self-hosting or system-level adequacy without unrestricted reflection. A direct cycle in which the same grant supplies its own only evidence remains outside the core. A separate cyclic extension may show that an ungrounded positive cycle stays unsupported only after declaring an immediate-consequence operator, selecting (for example) its least fixed point, and proving the required monotonicity and soundness conditions; no such result is assumed here.

Task 14C must audit Curry–Howard, refinement/dependent types, Hoare/program logics, proof-carrying code, certifying algorithms, graded/quantitative types, and related primary sources before claiming novelty. The likely project contribution is the integration of certificate-carrying computation with defeasible, domain-relative, quantitative licenses—not the underlying correspondence machinery by itself.

## Task 17 target statements frozen by this checkpoint

Tasks 15 and 16 must design toward the following theorem statements rather than choosing convenient encodings first.

1. **Architecture-neutral exact factorization.** For a declared input class and profile family `F`, an encoder/scorer plus deterministic decoder realizes every requested status exactly only if equal codes imply equal required `WF` observations and equal `V / ~_F` classes. Conversely, any code preserving those observations is status-sufficient. Diagnostic-preserving clients require the corresponding finer quotient.

2. **Margin-robust approximate realization.** If every learned continuous sufficient statistic has uniform error at most `delta`, then requests separated from all relevant support/refutation boundaries by more than the propagated error decode exactly; requests inside the uncertainty band are conservatively open. The result must state how multiple statistics, profiles, and certificate modes propagate error.

3. **Finite ReLU construction.** On a fixed finite-dimensional encoding, if the required real-valued atom statistics are continuous piecewise linear, a finite ReLU network with affine outputs computes them exactly under stated architecture, size/depth, and domain conventions. A fixed external, boundary-aware `WF + K_3` decoder then recovers the selected finite profile queries. Direct discrete `K_3` output and inclusive boundary decisions are not themselves continuous ReLU outputs.

4. **Polyhedral hard-seam characterization.** For affine experts on a conforming finite polyhedral complex, the assembled map is continuous exactly when expert traces agree on every relevant nonempty common face/intersection. Under that condition it is CPWL and receives an exact finite ReLU representation; disagreement on an accumulation face makes the hard assembly discontinuous and impossible for one ordinary continuous ReLU output, while an external discrete router or hard mixture of experts may still represent it.

5. **Finite versus expandable library limitation.** A fixed model-indexed output supports only its declared finite registry. An expandable design uses candidate-conditioned shared scoring, sparse set/pair reduction, and external identity/selection. Neither design converts evaluated-set non-domination into global closure.

6. **Dual-use joint-sufficiency and obstruction.** Characterize exactly when a representation is sufficient for both the declared license-query family and a downstream computation. Give a finite separator showing that an adequacy scalar alone generally cannot recover different payloads, costs, or traces, and state the scale/normalization conditions under which margin magnitude may safely be consumed.

7. **Annotated finite-plan ReLU realization.** If a fixed finite proof-erased annotated plan `(y_hat,b)` has CPWL primitives and grade transformers and all hard branches satisfy the required seam conditions, then the joint payload/grade map is CPWL and receives the exact finite ReLU construction. Proof/certificate terms remain externally checked; non-CPWL computation receives only a controlled approximation plus a verified envelope.

These are targets, not results already proved. Task 17 must verify the exact CPWL representation hypotheses, global-domain/extension convention, boundary cases, and size/depth attribution from primary sources.

## Architecture selection

Alternatives were screened by five criteria: preservation of the same semantic interface; a distinct theorem or empirical hypothesis; relevance to a surviving calculus property; matched-capacity/compute feasibility; and a failure criterion that is not merely “different accuracy.”

| architecture | nominated question | present decision |
|---|---|---|
| ReLU MLP plus symbolic decoder | Exact continuous-CPWL statistic realization, inspectable threshold margins, and activation geometry | **Reference architecture** |
| Monotone/lattice model | Does enforcing selected tolerance/refinement monotonicities improve extrapolation or calibration compared with learning them? | **Candidate comparator**, limited to coordinates whose monotonicity is actually proved |
| Hard mixture of experts / external hard router | Does native discontinuous routing improve seam-mismatch cases while preserving license masking and fallback? | **Candidate comparator** and direct foil for Task 17 |
| Neuro-symbolic decoder | Can exact `WF + K_3` aggregation prevent inconsistent aggregate status? | **Shared infrastructure**, not a comparator |
| Graph/set model | Does explicit plan-DAG structure preserve grade/certificate composition and generalize across structurally distinct plans better than flattening? | Project-author amendment supplies a hypothesis; Task 15 must operationalize it before Checkpoint C considers training |
| Maxout | Another continuous piecewise-linear family | Not selected: currently redundant with the ReLU representation question |

Checkpoint C may select at most one trained comparator for the toy experiment unless two distinct, adequately powered hypotheses survive. Architectural monotonicity and hard routing must not be advertised as general interpretability improvements without evidence.

## Mathematical economy

Checkpoint B adds no new principal carrier, truth value, reason enum, bridge type, or license predicate. The typed record inventory remains an elaboration. Operational atom families are instances of a finite typed atom set and valuation, not a growing ontology for the paper. `RelUndom` and `CertUndom` may be presented as modes of one comparison atom when doing so preserves their different evidence conditions. Diagnostics remain one atom-indexed dependent sum over `K_3`; human labels remain renderings.

Task 14B's query/read and event/write footprints are derived interfaces for the operational clauses, not new paper-level sorts. Current provenance nodes explain an assessment; typed key footprints determine which future writes could change it. The two should not be conflated.

The profile/query quotient may remove redundant status distinctions for a fixed consumer. Support-entailment can also remove redundant requirements when only grant preservation is sought, but it need not preserve `Refused` versus `Withheld` or the original diagnostic explanation. Any such normalization must keep the unquotiented address map for auditability. It is therefore an encoding option, not a new primitive definition.

## Risks after this checkpoint

1. Task 14B could become another schema exercise. Its success condition is a case-analysis theorem connecting actual atom reads and event writes to change-completeness, not a longer dependency record.
2. Task 17 could prove only that ReLU networks are universal approximators. That would not meet the frozen exact/robust/seam targets.
3. The query quotient could be misread as permission to discard diagnostics or as a neural-width lower bound. Both readings are now explicitly prohibited.
4. The experiment could become an omnibus benchmark too small to test any claim well. The tiered design is now required.
5. The two comparator candidates could create breadth without power. Checkpoint C must select by a concrete hypothesis and fairness budget.
6. The project may still overstate classical stability patterns or standard transport mathematics. Task 14B and Task 31 own explicit attribution/classification checks.
7. A proof-carrying computation claim could silently treat an empirical bound or neural confidence as a deductive proof. Task 14C must type proof terms, accepted empirical certificates, and world bridges separately.
8. Dual-use activations could turn arbitrary hidden features into post-hoc “adequacy” scores. Task 17 must prove joint sufficiency and scale semantics for the declared channels; otherwise the interpretation remains an empirical hypothesis.
9. System-level licensing could become circular self-endorsement. Only grounded, stratified assessment is in scope; genuine cycles require explicit fixed-point semantics.
